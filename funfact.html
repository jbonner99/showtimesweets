Theatre Fun Facts

  • Come From Away's character Beverly Bass was based on the real life first female captain of American Airlines. She has now seen almost 100 performaces of Come From Away.
  • The role of Leading Player in Pippin was never written with a defined gender. Ben Vereen originally played Leading player in 1972 and Patina Miller Played them in the 2013 reviaval. They both won Tonys for their performnaces making them the first two people of different genders to win Tonys for the same character.
  • Andrew Lloyd Webber's iconic The Phantom of the Opera is the longest-running Broadway show of all time. It has surpassed an incredible 10,000 performances
  • In order to qualify for Broadway theatre status, a theatre must have 500 seats and be located between 40th St. to 54th St., and from West of 6th Ave. to East of Eighth Ave., including Times Square.
  • The first Tony Awards was held in 1947 and was originally called- Antoinette Perry Awards for Excellence in Theatre. The name of the award was changed when Brok Pemberton handed out an award and called it a Tony - which was Antoinette's nickname.
  • You don't have to be on Broadway to be recognised as a Broadway theatre: you just have to have 500 or more seats. Theatres with 100-499 seats are known as off-Broadway (whether located on Broadway or not).
  • There have been almost 40 cast recordings of Les Miserables
  • Rent is loosly based on the 1896 opera La Boheme
